Internal A/B Testing Platform

Redesigning Booking.com's complex experimentation tool to streamline product development workflows and increase test success rates

Client

Booking.com

Role

Senior Product Designer (End-to-End Ownership)

Timeline

6 months (Aug 2018 - Jan 2019)

Team

Product Manager, 5 Engineers, Data Scientist

Project Overview

As a Senior Product Designer at Booking.com, I led the complete redesign of an internal A/B testing platform used by 500+ product managers and engineers globally. The existing tool had become increasingly complex and difficult to use, resulting in longer experiment setup times and higher error rates.

My role spanned the entire product design lifecycle: from initial discovery and user research to defining the product strategy, creating the information architecture, designing the interaction flows and UI, and collaborating with engineering throughout implementation.

The Challenge

The existing A/B testing tool had evolved organically over 8+ years, accumulating complexity without a coherent user experience. Product teams were spending excessive time configuring experiments, and technical errors were causing experiment failures that impacted business decisions.

My Role: Senior Product Designer

Unlike a UX Designer role focused primarily on user research and interface design, as a Senior Product Designer I owned the complete product experience from conception to delivery. This involved strategic decision-making, cross-functional leadership, and business impact measurement.

Product Strategy & Discovery

Conducted stakeholder interviews with product leadership to align on business goals. Analyzed 12 months of experiment data to identify pain points and opportunity areas. Defined success metrics in collaboration with the Product Manager.

User Research & Validation

Led contextual inquiries with 25 PMs and engineers across 4 offices. Conducted usability tests on existing workflows to quantify pain points. Created user journey maps and personas to guide design decisions.

Information Architecture

Restructured complex experiment configuration into logical, task-based flows. Created a scalable component system that could accommodate future feature additions. Defined navigation patterns that reduced cognitive load for frequent users.

Cross-functional Leadership

Partnered with Engineering leads on technical feasibility and implementation planning. Collaborated with Data Science on experiment analysis requirements. Facilitated workshops with stakeholders to align on scope and trade-offs.

Design Process & Methodology

I employed a structured, data-informed approach that balanced user needs with technical constraints and business objectives. The process was iterative, with regular checkpoints for validation and alignment.

Key Design Decisions

Progressive Disclosure: Instead of presenting all configuration options at once, I implemented a step-by-step wizard that revealed complexity based on user expertise level. Novice users could set up basic experiments quickly, while advanced users could access granular controls.

Contextual Guidance: Based on research showing that users often made errors in statistical parameter selection, I embedded inline education and validation directly in the interface, reducing configuration errors by 45%.

Reusable Templates: I analyzed the most common experiment types and created reusable templates that pre-populated standard configurations, saving users an average of 15 minutes per experiment setup.

Visual Design & System Thinking

The UI needed to handle complex data relationships while maintaining clarity. I extended Booking.com's design system with custom components for experiment visualization and statistical controls.

I created a consistent visual language for experiment states (draft, running, completed, paused) that worked across all platform views. This required close collaboration with the design systems team to ensure components were reusable across other internal tools.

Results & Business Impact

The redesigned platform launched to all product teams in Q1 2019. We measured impact across multiple dimensions:

Quantitative Impact

Efficiency Gains: Average experiment setup time decreased from 45 to 27 minutes (40% reduction), enabling teams to run more experiments per quarter.

Quality Improvement: Experiment success rates (statistically significant results without technical errors) increased from 65% to 85% (30% relative improvement).

Adoption & Satisfaction: User satisfaction scores increased from 3.2 to 4.5/5. Support tickets related to the platform decreased by 60%.

Strategic Impact

Accelerated Innovation: The reduced friction in experiment setup allowed product teams to test more hypotheses, accelerating the product innovation cycle.

Improved Decision Quality: With fewer experiment errors and clearer result visualization, product decisions became more data-informed and reliable.

Scalable Foundation: The modular design system I co-created with engineering became the foundation for future internal tools, reducing development time for subsequent projects by approximately 40%.

Reflection & Learnings

This project exemplified the Senior Product Designer role at scale: translating ambiguous requirements into a coherent product strategy, making complex technical concepts accessible, and driving measurable business impact through design.

Key Learnings:

  • Early technical collaboration is critical for complex tools. Involving engineers during the discovery phase helped identify architectural constraints early.
  • Quantitative data complements qualitative insights. Combining usage analytics with user interviews revealed patterns that neither would show alone.
  • Designing for expertise progression requires different patterns than consumer products. Advanced users need efficiency while novices need guidance.
  • Measuring design impact requires baseline metrics. Establishing clear KPIs before redesign allowed us to demonstrate tangible business value.
